# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We pledge to make participation in the PaintedClouds Hub community a
harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible
or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and
expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality,
personal appearance, race, caste, color, religion, or sexual identity and
orientation.

We will act in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive,
and healthy community.

## Our Standards

Positive behavior includes empathy, respect for different viewpoints,
constructive feedback, accountability, and a focus on the health of the whole
community.

Unacceptable behavior includes:

- sexualized language or imagery and unwelcome sexual attention or advances;
- trolling, insults, derogatory comments, or personal or political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without explicit permission;
- threats, intimidation, or advocacy of harm;
- other conduct reasonably considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these
standards. They may remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and
other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct and will explain
moderation decisions when appropriate and safe.

## Scope

This Code applies in project spaces and when a person officially represents the
project in public. It does not give the project authority over unrelated private
conduct, except where that conduct creates a credible safety risk in project
spaces.

## Reporting

Do not report sensitive conduct incidents in a public issue. Use the
repository's private vulnerability-reporting form under the GitHub Security tab
and begin the summary with `Code of Conduct report`. If private reporting is not
enabled, preserve the report privately until the repository owner publishes a
confidential contact channel.

Maintainers will protect the privacy and safety of reporters and affected
people as far as reasonably possible. A maintainer who is involved in a report
must recuse themselves. Knowingly false or retaliatory reports are themselves a
violation, but a report made in good faith is not a violation merely because it
cannot be substantiated.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Consequences are proportionate to context, impact, history, and willingness to
correct behavior. They may include a private correction, warning, temporary
restriction, or permanent ban. Serious threats, harassment, disclosure of
private information, or repeated violations may result in immediate removal.
